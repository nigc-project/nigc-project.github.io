National Indian Gaming Commission

The National Indian Gaming Commission regulates Indian Gaming, or gambling on Native American reservations, and was formed in 1988. It is a commission made up of a whole three full time members, and several other regional members who manage compliance in different states.

The members are:

  • A chairman, appointed by the U.S. President
  • Two associate members appointed by the Secretary of the Interior
